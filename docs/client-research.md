Treats By Tayjah is a small baking business based in Manchester M14. The Linktree describes the business as specialising in custom cakes, cupcakes, cookies and more, with the line "Treats That Taste As Good As They Look!" It links to Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, Threads, and an order enquiry form.

## Business Position

Treats By Tayjah sits in the custom celebration dessert category rather than the walk-in bakery category. The strongest immediate positioning is:

"A Manchester-based custom treats studio for celebration cakes, cupcakes, cookies and dessert moments designed to look beautiful and feel personal."

This keeps the brand premium and occasion-led without claiming anything unverified about delivery, dietary options, opening hours, or product availability.

## Local Market Context

Manchester has a crowded bakery and dessert scene, with competitors ranging from home-based custom bakers to established bakeries with ordering systems, delivery coverage, wholesale options, and storefronts. South Manchester and M14-adjacent areas such as Fallowfield, Withington, Rusholme, Didsbury, Chorlton and central Manchester are likely search areas for customers looking for birthday cakes, cupcakes, treat boxes and event desserts.

Customer intent is high but often last-minute. Buyers usually need confidence around visual style, availability, flavour options, notice periods, collection or delivery options, and how to secure a booking. The website can win by making the enquiry journey calmer and clearer than social media alone.

## Competitor Landscape

### The Tiny Baker

Appears positioned as a Manchester handmade bakery with custom cakes, cupcakes and treat boxes. Its website foregrounds an easy ordering journey: tell us the vibe, choose flavours, reserve your date. This is a useful model for reducing enquiry friction.

### AiryFairy Cake Co

An established South Manchester bakery with broad Manchester/Cheshire delivery, corporate/branded events and wholesale capability. It competes more strongly on scale, longevity and delivery coverage. Treats By Tayjah can differentiate through a more personal, boutique, social-first custom feel.

### Bakerchick

Manchester cake and cupcake business with online ordering language, gallery-led browsing and bespoke order prompts. It competes on convenience and direct website clarity.

### Alex's Bakery

Manchester city centre bakery with a visible location, broad range and search discoverability. It competes for urgent or central Manchester cake searches.

### Bakerdays

National personalised cake delivery competitor with strong SEO for "cake delivery Manchester". It competes on convenience and catalogue choice, not local maker personality.

## Customer Journey

### 1. Discovery

Customers likely find the business through Instagram, TikTok, referrals, Google Maps, Facebook, or local searches such as "custom cakes Manchester" and "cupcakes Manchester M14".

Website opportunity: The homepage should quickly confirm what Treats By Tayjah makes, where it is based, and how to enquire.

### 2. Inspiration

Customers browse previous designs to decide whether the style matches their event. Social posts probably do much of this work today.

Website opportunity: Create gallery filters such as cakes, cupcakes, cookies, treat boxes, birthdays, baby showers, graduations, events, and colour/style if enough assets exist.

### 3. Confidence

Before enquiring, customers need to know whether their date, flavour, theme, quantity and collection/delivery needs might be possible.

Website opportunity: Add "How to order" with a simple 3-4 step flow: share your date and idea, confirm details, secure your slot, collect or receive handover details. Use placeholders where logistics are not verified.

### 4. Enquiry

Customers submit the form or DM. The current Linktree form already supports enquiry-led ordering.

Website opportunity: Put one primary CTA throughout the site: "Start an enquiry". Secondary CTA: "View recent bakes".

### 5. Booking

The Linktree says orders are confirmed after a response and a 50% deposit. This can be repeated only if the client confirms it should appear on the website.

Website opportunity: Use clear, non-legal copy: "Your order is confirmed once details are agreed and the required deposit has been received." Avoid inventing payment methods or policies.

### 6. Pickup, Delivery or Event Use

Specific options are not verified.

Website opportunity: Use safe wording: "Collection/delivery details are confirmed during enquiry." Do not claim delivery radius, fees, courier options, or exact collection address.

## Trend Signals Relevant to the Website

### Bento and Mini Celebration Cakes

UK bakery trend coverage in 2026 points to bento cakes and smaller personalised formats continuing to grow, especially among younger consumers seeking playful, bespoke, giftable desserts. If Treats By Tayjah offers this format, it should become a featured product. If not, the website can still borrow the trend's language: personal, giftable, photo-ready, made for small moments.

### Vintage and Lambeth-Inspired Piping

Vintage-style cakes, ornate piping, heart cakes and "old-money" celebration aesthetics remain visible across wedding and celebration cake trend coverage. If this matches Tayjah's actual portfolio, the homepage and gallery should highlight piping detail and tactile buttercream texture.

### Everyday Indulgence and Small Wins

2026 bakery trend reports point beyond birthdays and holidays toward self-gifting, small wins and everyday celebrations. This is useful for cupcakes, cookies and treat boxes: not every order has to be a major event.

### Texture, Flavour Hybrids and Nostalgia

Trend coverage highlights layered textures, nostalgic flavours, dessert mashups and flavour storytelling. Website copy can ask customers for "flavours, colours, theme and any inspiration images" without inventing a menu.

### Social-First Visual Buying

For custom cakes and treats, customers often buy the look before they understand the process. The website should therefore be image-led, with fast access to proof of work, then clear enquiry steps.

## Website Opportunities

### Homepage

The homepage should be an immediate product experience, not a generic landing page. First viewport should show real treats, the brand name, Manchester M14 signal, product range and one enquiry CTA.

Recommended hero message:

"Custom cakes, cupcakes and cookies for Manchester celebrations."

Supporting line:

"Beautifully finished treats made to match your moment, from birthdays and baby showers to thoughtful gifts and event tables."

CTA:

"Start an enquiry"

Secondary CTA:

"View recent bakes"

### Gallery

A gallery is the strongest conversion asset. Use real images only. If the client has enough content, sort by occasion, product and style.

### Product Pages

Use flexible, non-price-led pages:

- Custom Cakes
- Cupcakes
- Cookies
- Treat Boxes and Extras, if confirmed
- Events and Occasions, if confirmed

Each page should explain what information to send: date, occasion, servings or quantity, theme, colour palette, flavour preferences, inspiration images, collection/delivery needs.

### How To Order

Make the enquiry process feel simple:

1. Share your date and idea.
2. Confirm design, flavour and quantity.
3. Secure your booking once details and deposit are confirmed.
4. Final collection or handover details are agreed before the date.

### Trust and Compliance

Include a Food Hygiene Rating area using the FSA embed if the client wants it displayed. Because the FSA recommends embed code that updates automatically, this is better than a static badge.

### Local SEO Structure

Create locally relevant copy without exposing the private address:

"Based in Manchester M14 and taking enquiries for custom celebration treats across the local area."

Potential area mentions should be phrased carefully:

"Popular for customers enquiring from areas such as Fallowfield, Withington, Rusholme, Didsbury, Chorlton and wider Manchester, subject to availability and agreed collection/delivery details."

Only include those areas if the client is comfortable with them.

## Strategic Recommendation

Build the website around three promises: beauty, clarity and confidence. Social media can keep selling the visual dream, while the website should make ordering feel premium, organised and reassuring. The best near-term opportunity is not to out-scale bigger Manchester bakeries, but to make Treats By Tayjah feel like the most personal, polished and easy-to-enquire custom treats studio for M14 and nearby Manchester celebration searches.
