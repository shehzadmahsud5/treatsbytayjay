# Treats By Tayjah Brand System

Brand direction date: 24 May 2026  
Client: Treats By Tayjah  
Project stage: Brand direction for website planning only.

## Brand Positioning

Treats By Tayjah should feel like a premium Manchester custom treats studio: personal enough to feel made by a real baker, polished enough to trust with milestone moments.

Positioning statement:

"Treats By Tayjah creates custom cakes, cupcakes and cookies for Manchester celebrations, designed with care, finished beautifully and made to feel personal from first enquiry to final reveal."

Core differentiation:

- Boutique and personal, not mass-market.
- Visual and celebration-led, not commodity bakery.
- Social-first style with a clearer, calmer website ordering journey.
- Based in Manchester M14, with local relevance but without exposing a private address.

## Target Audience

Primary audience:

- Manchester-based customers planning birthdays, baby showers, graduations, family events, parties, gifts and small celebrations.
- Social media-aware buyers who care about how the cake or treat box photographs.
- Customers who want custom work but need a simple, reassuring enquiry process.

Secondary audience:

- Event organisers, small businesses and local creatives seeking branded cupcakes, cookies or dessert table pieces, pending client confirmation.
- Friends and family buying thoughtful edible gifts.

Audience mindset:

"I have a date and an idea. I need someone who can make it look special, taste good, and guide me through what is possible."

## Brand Personality

- Polished: neat, composed, premium and detail-conscious.
- Warm: approachable, kind and easy to speak to.
- Celebratory: joyful without being childish.
- Creative: responsive to themes, colours and personal moments.
- Trustworthy: clear about enquiries, availability and confirmation.

## Tone Of Voice

The voice should be warm, confident and sensory. It should sound like a premium small business, not a corporate bakery.

Use:

- "made for your moment"
- "custom treats"
- "beautifully finished"
- "share your date, theme and inspiration"
- "designed around your celebration"
- "subject to availability"
- "collection/delivery details confirmed during enquiry"

Avoid:

- Cheap or discount-led language.
- Overpromising speed, delivery, flavours or dietary options.
- Fake urgency.
- Claims like "best in Manchester" unless supported by a credible award or source.
- Exact policies unless provided by the client.

Example tone:

"Tell us the date, the occasion and the look you have in mind. We will come back to you with availability and the next steps to bring your treats together."

## Colour Palette

The palette should feel premium, sweet and editorial, with enough contrast for a polished website. Avoid an all-pink look; the brand needs softness, depth and food warmth.

### Core Palette

- Buttercream: `#FFF7ED`  
  Warm page background and soft sections.

- Ganache: `#2B1F1A`  
  Primary text, footer and high-contrast anchors.

- Raspberry Glaze: `#C94F6D`  
  Primary CTA, active states and small accents.

- Pistachio Silk: `#A7BFA3`  
  Secondary accent, badges, subtle backgrounds.

- Toasted Sugar: `#D8A45F`  
  Highlights, dividers, small premium details.

- Porcelain: `#FFFFFF`  
  Clean content surfaces and image breathing room.

### Supporting Tints

- Rose Cream: `#F7DDE5`
- Cocoa Mist: `#7A6258`
- Sage Dust: `#E7EFE3`
- Champagne: `#F4E1C1`

### Accessibility Notes

- Use Ganache text on Buttercream, Porcelain, Champagne and Rose Cream.
- Use Porcelain text on Ganache.
- Use Raspberry Glaze for buttons with Porcelain text only if contrast is checked during design.
- Do not put pale pink text on cream backgrounds.

## Typography Direction

The typography should balance boutique charm with readability.

Headline direction:

- Elegant serif with soft contrast.
- Suggested families: Playfair Display, Cormorant Garamond, Lora or Fraunces.
- Use for hero, section headers and selected pull quotes.

Body direction:

- Clean modern sans serif.
- Suggested families: Inter, Manrope, Nunito Sans or Source Sans 3.
- Use for navigation, body copy, forms, FAQs and product descriptions.

Accent direction:

- Optional handwritten or script accent used sparingly for small labels only.
- Suggested usage: one or two words such as "custom", "celebrate", or "Tayjah".
- Avoid using script for paragraphs, buttons or important instructions.

## Visual Style

The website should feel like a premium dessert lookbook with an ordering path built in.

Image style:

- Real product photography should lead.
- Use close crops of piping, crumbs, frosting textures, cookie detail and boxed arrangements.
- Show hands, ribbons, cake boxes and celebration tables where available.
- Keep backgrounds clean: marble, white, soft cream, muted linen or simple party settings.

Layout style:

- Editorial, spacious, image-led.
- Rounded corners can be subtle, no more than 8px unless matching future design rules.
- Avoid heavy decorative cards. Let product images carry the brand.
- Use slim dividers, small labels and refined spacing instead of busy decoration.

Motion style:

- Slow, soft and satisfying.
- Movements should feel like frosting being piped, ribbon being pulled, a box being opened.
- Avoid bouncy, novelty or childish animation.

## CTA Wording

Primary CTAs:

- Start an enquiry
- Send an enquiry
- Enquire about a date
- Plan my treats

Secondary CTAs:

- View recent bakes
- Browse cakes
- Browse cupcakes
- See how ordering works

Micro CTAs:

- Share your inspiration
- Check availability
- Ask about a custom order

Avoid:

- Buy now, unless e-commerce is added.
- Book instantly, unless instant booking exists.
- Order today, unless stock and same-day fulfilment are confirmed.
- Get delivery, unless delivery details are confirmed.

## Do And Don't Rules

### Do

- Use the confirmed phrase "Treats That Taste As Good As They Look!" as a brand line.
- Mention Manchester M14 as a location signal.
- Keep the enquiry process visible on every page.
- Use real product imagery wherever possible.
- Write copy that invites ideas: date, occasion, theme, colours, flavours, quantity and inspiration images.
- Use placeholders for unknown logistics.
- Include food hygiene trust information if the client approves.

### Don't

- Do not publish the exact address.
- Do not invent prices.
- Do not invent opening hours.
- Do not invent delivery fees or service radius.
- Do not claim same-day, next-day or rush orders.
- Do not claim halal, vegan, gluten-free, nut-free, dairy-free or allergen-safe options.
- Do not create fake reviews or testimonials.
- Do not call the business award-winning unless evidence is provided.
- Do not imply every inspiration image can be exactly copied.

## Cupcake Scroll Animation Direction

The cupcake scroll animation should feel premium, tactile and gently theatrical. Imagine the user scrolling through a reveal sequence where a cupcake is being finished by hand.

Suggested feeling:

- Slow frosting swirl appears or rotates subtly as the page moves.
- Tiny sugar pearls or crumbs drift into place with restraint.
- The cupcake wrapper rises or settles like a product being placed on a counter.
- A final soft highlight catches the frosting at the moment the CTA appears.

Motion principles:

- Smooth, creamy easing.
- No fast spins.
- No confetti explosion unless used as a very subtle final accent.
- Keep the cupcake realistic or beautifully stylised, not cartoonish.
- The animation should support the order journey, not distract from the enquiry CTA.

Best placement:

- Between hero and product sections as a signature brand moment.
- Or alongside "How your order comes together", where each scroll stage reveals a step.

Accessibility:

- Respect reduced-motion preferences.
- Do not rely on animation to communicate essential ordering information.
- Keep text readable and separate from moving elements.

## Brand Protection Notes

- Keep logo, name and tagline consistent across website, social profiles and enquiry form.
- Use the same CTA language on website and Linktree where possible.
- Confirm permission before reusing social media images.
- If embedding the Food Hygiene Rating, use the official FSA embed so the rating stays current.
- Keep a simple claims checklist before launch: no unverified prices, policies, address, hours, delivery, dietary or allergen claims.

## Brand Summary

Treats By Tayjah should feel sweet but grown-up, premium but warm, creative but reliable. The website should make customers think: "This will look beautiful, the process is clear, and my celebration is in careful hands."
